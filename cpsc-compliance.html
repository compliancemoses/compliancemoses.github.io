The Situation

CPSC Enforcement
Requires Exact Response.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission enforces federal safety standards for virtually every consumer product imported or sold in the United States. A CPSC enforcement action — whether a recall, a Section 15(b) notice, a civil penalty, or a compliance order — requires a precise, documented response. Improvised responses make situations worse.

What we know: CPSC enforcement priorities, recall coordination procedures, and the documentation that resolves each violation type are specific to the product category and the nature of the hazard. We manage CPSC matters end to end — from initial notice through corrective action closure.

Questions

What Clients Ask First

What is the difference between a GCC and a CPC?
A General Conformity Certificate (GCC) is required for general use consumer products. A Children's Product Certificate (CPC) is required for products designed or intended for children under 12 and must be backed by third-party testing at a CPSC-accepted laboratory. Amazon requires both for the applicable product categories.
When must I report to CPSC under Section 15(b)?
Section 15(b) reporting is triggered when a company has information that reasonably suggests a product contains a defect that could create a substantial product hazard, or fails to comply with a consumer product safety rule. The reporting obligation is strict — companies that fail to report face significant civil penalties.
Does a CPSC issue affect my Amazon account?
Yes. CPSC enforcement actions, recall notices, and even Section 15(b) reports can trigger Amazon marketplace enforcement independently. We coordinate CPSC compliance with Amazon listing protection where both are implicated.
We import from China — can you coordinate with our supplier?
Yes. CPSC documentation requirements often require input from the manufacturer — test reports, production records, material certifications. We coordinate across the supply chain to assemble the complete compliance package.
