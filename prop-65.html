California Proposition 65 allows private enforcement parties to file lawsuits against businesses selling products that expose consumers to listed chemicals without the required warning. The 60-day notice is not a courtesy — it is the opening move in a legal sequence that ends in court if not resolved.

What we know: A concentrated group of enforcement firms file the majority of all Prop 65 notices. Their targets, their chemical focus areas, their procedural patterns — all known. That intelligence shapes how we respond on your behalf.

Questions

What Clients Ask First

Can I challenge the notice instead of settling?
Yes — and sometimes that is the correct strategy. Notices with procedural defects, incorrect chemical identifications, or noticing parties lacking proper standing can be challenged. We assess every notice for challenge viability before recommending a path.
What does early resolution typically cost?
Resolutions reached within the first 30 days are consistently less expensive than those negotiated closer to the deadline or after litigation commences. The specifics depend on the noticing party, the chemical, and your sales exposure. We provide a clear picture after reviewing your notice.
We have received notices before and handled them ourselves. Why use Moses Compliance?
Companies that receive repeat notices are tracked. A pattern of prior notices affects your negotiating position on each subsequent one. Moses Compliance manages your compliance posture across notices — not just one at a time.
Does this affect our Amazon listings?
It can. Prop 65 enforcement actions are public record. Amazon's compliance systems monitor these filings. We coordinate Prop 65 resolution with Amazon listing protection where both are at risk simultaneously.
