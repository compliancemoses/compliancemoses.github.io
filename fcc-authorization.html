The Situation

Every Electronic Product
Needs FCC Authorization.

The Federal Communications Commission requires authorization for virtually every electronic device capable of emitting radio frequency energy — which includes most consumer electronics, wireless devices, Bluetooth accessories, and IoT products. Selling or importing without the required authorization is a federal violation, and Amazon actively enforces FCC requirements on its marketplace.

What we know: FCC authorization pathways — Certification, Supplier Declaration of Conformity, and Verification — each apply to different product categories and emission types. The path that applies to your product determines the documentation, the timeline, and the cost. We identify the correct path and execute it.

Questions

What Clients Ask First

My product already has an FCC ID from the manufacturer — is that enough?
Not always. If you have modified the product, changed its housing, combined it with other RF components, or are selling it under your own brand, the original FCC authorization may not cover your version. We assess whether the existing authorization applies or whether a new authorization is required.
What is the difference between FCC Certification and SDoC?
FCC Certification (which generates an FCC ID) is required for intentional radiators — devices that deliberately emit RF energy, such as Bluetooth and Wi-Fi products. SDoC applies to unintentional radiators — devices that emit RF as a byproduct of operation. The correct pathway depends on your specific product.
Amazon is asking for FCC authorization documentation — what do I submit?
Amazon requires either the FCC ID grant of equipment authorization or the SDoC letter with the supporting technical file, depending on the product. The documentation must be current, product-specific, and correctly formatted. We prepare exactly what Amazon requires.
Do you handle FCC authorization for products manufactured in China?
Yes. The majority of electronic products we handle are manufactured in China. We coordinate with the manufacturer to obtain the required test data and technical documentation, and manage the authorization process from submission through issuance.
