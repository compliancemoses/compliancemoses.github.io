Questions

What Clients Ask First

How long do I have to respond?
FDA typically provides a narrow window — the exact timeframe depends on the type of notice and the district. Contact us immediately upon receiving any FDA detention or refusal notice. Do not wait to understand the document before reaching out.
Can a refused shipment be released?
Yes — in many cases. A formal admissibility submission with the correct documentation can reverse a refusal and secure release of your goods. The strength of the submission determines the outcome. A generic response almost never works.
What triggers Import Alert placement?
Repeated violations, failure to respond to detention notices, and serious adulteration or misbranding findings are common triggers. Import Alert placement means every future shipment from your company is automatically detained. Removal requires a formal compliance petition.
Do you work with manufacturers in China?
Yes. Many FDA refusals originate at the manufacturer level — labeling errors, missing documentation, ingredient or formulation issues. We coordinate directly across the supply chain, including with Chinese manufacturers, to assemble the complete submission.
